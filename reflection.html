Speculative Reflection

What I want my symposium project to become

This section is intentionally forward-looking. It captures what I currently hope to present at the All-College Symposium after several more years of coursework, research, and off-campus experience.

Presentation direction

The presentation I hope to give

In a few years, I would like my symposium presentation to center on a tracking-based hockey research project that shows how public player evaluation can move beyond simplified advanced metrics. Right now, the version I imagine most clearly is an Ice Control Value framework that measures who can reach dangerous space first, whether the puck can realistically be moved there, and what that space is worth if possession is gained. I do not want the project to be impressive only because it is technical. I want it to be understandable to people outside hockey analytics, so that the presentation becomes a case study in how data, interpretation, and access shape what kinds of claims we make about performance.

Audience takeaway

What I want the audience to leave with

I hope the symposium version would combine a poster-style argument with visual maps or a live demo so the audience can actually see how spacing, support, and passing access change across a play. More than anything, I want people to come away with the idea that analytics is not just about producing one stronger number. It is also about deciding what to measure, what remains invisible, how confident we should be in model outputs, and how we can communicate uncertainty honestly. If I can show that a hockey project can raise those broader questions clearly, then it would feel like a genuine Pathway culmination rather than only a sports presentation.

What that symposium might include

I expect the final form to evolve, but this is the structure I can imagine right now.

1

A clear public-facing claim

The opening would explain the gap between popular public metrics and richer tracking-based representations of performance.

2

Visual control-access-value maps

I would like to show spatial visualizations that make the model interpretable instead of leaving the audience with a black-box score.

3

Broader pathway takeaway

The project would close by reflecting on what the work says about public knowledge, data authority, and the ethics of quantitative argument.
