Global-Local Engagement

NHL Stats Research & Development internship

My planned global-local engagement is my Summer 2026 internship as a Stats Research & Development Intern with the National Hockey League in New York City.

Specific experience

Why this internship is a strong fit

This internship fits my pathway because it places me in exactly the kind of environment where questions about data collection, tracking systems, model design, validation, and communication are actively lived rather than discussed only in theory. I will be working in person in Manhattan with the NHL’s Stats R&D group, which makes the experience locally concrete, but it also connects to a global sport, international player development, and public-private differences in data access that matter well beyond one office or one city.

Why it matters for the pathway

How it sharpens my animating question

My animating question asks how richer tracking and vision-based information can create more informative and transparent ways of evaluating hockey performance. An NHL R&D setting is ideal because it exposes me to how high-level analytical questions are framed in practice and how much design work goes into making a model useful. Just as importantly, the internship will make me think harder about the line between private analytical advantage and public knowledge, which is one of the central Data, Information and Society themes inside my pathway.

What I hope to gain

This engagement is not separate from the pathway. It is where the pathway becomes real.

Technical

Better research habits

I want to improve how I scope projects, validate models, document assumptions, and communicate results in a professional research setting.

Intellectual

Stronger questions about data access

Seeing how richer information is used in practice will make me more precise about what public metrics can and cannot claim, and why transparency matters.

Future impact

Material for later research

The internship will support later independent work, including my planned Ice Control Value research direction and future symposium presentation.

Connection back to Conn

Bringing the experience home

I want to return with sharper technical judgment, stronger examples for teaching and mentoring, and clearer direction for how my courses, projects, and pathway can stay connected.

Pathway note

Why this counts as global-local engagement

Even though the internship is based in New York City, the work connects local practice with broader global questions about data systems, analytics labor, international sport, and what kinds of knowledge become public. That makes it a strong fit for the Pathway’s emphasis on meaningful engagement beyond the classroom.
